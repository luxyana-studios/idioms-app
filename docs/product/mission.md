# Product Mission

## Core Idea

Idioms is an app for discovering, understanding, and comparing idiomatic expressions across languages.

Most apps treat idioms as vocabulary to memorize. Idioms treats them as **cultural puzzles to understand** — by making the gap between what an expression literally says and what it actually means visible, explorable, and connected across languages.

---

## USP

> "Idioms doesn't just tell you what an expression means — it shows you how that same idea is expressed across cultures, in their own words."

---

## The Three-Layer Reveal

The core experience of the app. Every idiom card surfaces three layers:

| Layer | Example |
|---|---|
| **Expression** | Piece of cake |
| **Literal meaning** | A slice of a baked dessert |
| **Idiomatic meaning** | Something very easy to do |
| **Cross-language equivalent (ES)** | Pan comido — "eaten bread" |
| **Cross-language equivalent (DE)** | Kinderspiel — "child's play" |
| **Cross-language equivalent (FR)** | C'est du gâteau — "it's cake" |

Cross-language equivalents are tappable — each opens that idiom's own card, creating a **navigable web of idioms across languages**. This is the discovery mechanic and the product moat.

---

## Target User

Someone learning a language, or curious about language and culture, who wants to understand idioms deeply — not just memorize them. They care about the *why*, not just the *what*.

---

## Competitive Differentiation

| Other apps | Idioms |
|---|---|
| Treat idioms as vocab | Treats them as cultural puzzles |
| Memorize the meaning | Understand *why* meaning differs from wording |
| Single language | Multi-language, cross-cultural from day one |
| Flashcard UX | Reveal / discovery UX |
| No cross-language mapping | Equivalent idiom in every supported language |
